TikTok and Nicaragua Are Fueling African Migration to New York

The number of West African migrants arriving in the United States through Nicaragua has been increasing exponentially over the past year. For many, the Central American country has become a springboard to the US.


African migrants leaving Randall’s Island shelter after the city capped placement to 30 days. Nov. 3, 2023 / Photo: Naoufal Enhari

By Naoufal Enhari


November, 30, 2023


When Sayed Kotbo attempted to reach New York last month from Senegal, he knew personally at least six people, including his cousin, who arrived earlier this year to the United States after hearing on TikTok about a new route via Nicaragua.

Kotbo, 28, said he had seen several videos and social media posts praising this “safer” and “faster” route, which allows people in West Africa to fly without a visa to Nicaragua, in Central America, before trekking their way north to the US border.

“It’s the best route right now,” he said in French after arriving in New York City at the end of October, two weeks after leaving Dakar, the capital of Senegal.

In fiscal year 2023, the number of new proceedings in deportation cases for Senegalese nationals filed in US immigration courts increased by nearly 500%, reaching 11,408 cases, compared to 1,958 cases a year before, according to the Transactional Records Access Clearinghouse (TRAC) at Syracuse University.



“The younger generations of West Africans are dreaming of America,” said Boukary Sawadogo, an associate professor at the City College of New York, who has researched and written about African immigration and diaspora in the United States.

In an interview, he argues that a growing number of young Africans are abandoning Europe as a migration destination in favor of the United States.
